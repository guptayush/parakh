# Produce Scanner — Cue Cards

**Rule:** every check fits in 3 seconds, with empty hands, while the vendor watches. No cotton, no water, no asking him to cut it open.

Source research: `produce-scanner-research.md`.

---

## 1. Tomato
**Do:** Sniff the green stem cap.
**Buy if:** Sweet, faintly leafy smell.
**Skip if:** No smell, or sour. Cracks near the stem. Sunken dark patch on the bottom.

---

## 2. Watermelon
**Do:** Find the patch where it sat on the ground.
**Buy if:** Patch is creamy yellow.
**Skip if:** Patch is white or pale green (picked early). Shiny rind. Stem tendril still green.

---

## 3. Mango
**Do:** Sniff the stem end.
**Buy if:** Strong fruity, sweet smell.
**Skip if:** No smell, or chemical smell. Patchy color with sharp green/yellow borders. Black burnt-looking spots.

> Red ≠ ripe. Red is the variety. Ignore it.

---

## 4. Banana
**Do:** Look at the stem vs the fruit.
**Buy if:** Yellow fruit + dried brown stem. Brown freckles on the peel.
**Skip if:** Yellow fruit + green stem (carbide). Spotless plastic-yellow peel.

---

## 5. Apple
**Do:** Look at the yellow-green background under the red blush.
**Buy if:** Yellow or yellow-green undertone.
**Skip if:** Bright green undertone (under-ripe). Soft brown bruise. Skin dents under your thumb.

> Red intensity is not ripeness. The undertone is.

---

## 6. Onion
**Do:** Squeeze the neck (top) between two fingers.
**Buy if:** Dry, tight, papery.
**Skip if:** Squishy, damp, or wet. Black soot under the skin. Long green sprouts.

---

## 7. Potato
**Do:** Look for green tinge anywhere on the skin.
**Buy if:** Plain brown skin, firm to a thumb-press.
**Skip if:** Any green patch (= solanine, toxic). Long sprouts. Soft or rubbery skin.

---

## 8. Spinach (Palak)
**Do:** Bend a stem.
**Buy if:** Crisp, snaps clean. Deep green leaves, dry to touch.
**Skip if:** Slimy film on leaves. Yellowing. Sour smell. Suspiciously glossy uniform green (likely dyed).

---

## 9. Cauliflower
**Do:** Lift two same-size heads, pick the heavier one.
**Buy if:** Compact creamy-white curd, fresh green wrapper leaves.
**Skip if:** Loose ricey curd. Dark sunken patches. Yellow wilted leaves. Sulfur smell.

---

## 10. Brinjal (Eggplant)
**Do:** Thumb-press near the stem.
**Buy if:** Skin springs back. Glossy taut surface, fresh green calyx.
**Skip if:** Dent stays. Wrinkled dull skin. Brown calyx.

---

## 11. Carrot
**Do:** Bend it gently.
**Buy if:** Resists, then snaps clean with a sharp crack.
**Skip if:** Bends without snapping (water-lost). Green shoulders at the top. Hairy white rootlets.

---

## 12. Papaya
**Do:** Sniff the stem end.
**Buy if:** Sweet, musky, slightly floral.
**Skip if:** No smell, or alcoholic/sour. Patchy color with sharp borders. Mushy in some spots, rock-hard in others.

---

## 13. Grapes
**Do:** Lift the bunch and shake it gently.
**Buy if:** Berries hold tight to green pliable stems.
**Skip if:** Many berries fall. Brown brittle stems. Vinegar smell. Sticky brown leaks.

> The silvery dust on the skin is the plant's own wax. Don't wipe it off — it means freshness.

---

## The 4 actions that cover everything

If you remember nothing else:

1. **Sniff the stem end** → tomato, mango, papaya, watermelon
2. **Check the stem color or condition** → banana, grapes, brinjal, tomato
3. **Press or bend** → apple, brinjal, carrot, spinach, potato
4. **Lift and compare weight** → watermelon, cauliflower, onion, apple

---

## Three things to ignore

- **Red color on apples and mangoes.** That's the variety, not ripeness.
- **Watermelon thump test.** Field spot beats it.
- **White dust on grapes.** Plant wax. Good sign, not bad.

---

## At-home checks (don't try at the stall)

These are real but need a kitchen and a minute:

- **Apple wax** — scratch with a knife after washing; thick white flake = synthetic wax.
- **Watermelon dye** — once cut, dab cotton on the red flesh. Pink stain on cotton = erythrosine.
- **Spinach / palak dye** — wipe a leaf with damp white tissue. Green color on tissue = malachite green.
- **Carrot dye** — wipe with damp white cloth. Heavy orange transfer = Rhodamine B.
- **Cauliflower worms** — soak florets in salt water 10 min; worms float out.
- **Grapes pesticide** — soak in baking soda or 2% salt water 5 min, rinse.

---

## What we won't claim to detect

- **Oxytocin in vegetables** — real but lab-only. No camera or finger-test works.
- **Pesticide residue** — lab-only. We can only teach washing.
- **Wax type** — scratch test flags "heavy wax", not whether it's legal carnauba or illegal polyethylene.
